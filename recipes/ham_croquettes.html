Spanish Ham Croquettes

Description

Spanish Ham Croquettes, known as Croquetas de Jamón , are a classic tapa in Spain. They are crispy on the outside and creamy on the inside, made with béchamel sauce and finely chopped cured ham. They are commonly served as an appetizer or snack.

Ingredients (4-6 servings)

  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk (warm)
  • 1 cup finely chopped Spanish cured ham
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the béchamel

    2. Melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the flour and stir constantly for 2-3 minutes to form a smooth paste.

  2. Add the milk

    3. Gradually add the warm milk while stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Cook until the mixture thickens and becomes smooth and creamy.

  3. Add the ham

    4. Stir in the chopped ham and salt. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

  4. Chill the mixture

    5. Transfer the mixture to a shallow dish and refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours, or until firm.

  5. Shape the croquettes

    6. Once firm, shape the mixture into small cylinders or balls.

  6. Coat the croquettes

    7. Roll each croquette in flour, dip into beaten egg, then coat with breadcrumbs.

  7. Fry

    8. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan. Fry the croquettes in batches until golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve hot as an appetizer or tapa.
  • Pair with aioli or a light salad.
  • Best enjoyed fresh and crispy.
